Important terms

Renewable energy is energy that comes from sources that can be replenished naturally over time, such as solar, wind, geothermal, hydro, and biomass. These sources of energy do not run out and are considered to be sustainable and environmentally friendly alternatives to non-renewable sources of energy, such as fossil fuels.
Surface temperature is the temperature of the Earth's surface and is influenced by natural and human factors. It is measured using instruments such as thermometers and is important for understanding climate patterns and climate change.
Wind velocity refers to the speed and direction of wind flow, typically measured in meters per second or miles per hour. Wind velocity is influenced by a variety of factors, including differences in air pressure, the rotation of the Earth, and the surface characteristics of the land or water over which the wind is blowing.
Energy that is generated from the sun's radiation and converted into usable electricity using solar panels.
Energy that is generated from the wind's kinetic energy and converted into usable electricity using wind turbines.
Energy that is generated from the Earth's heat and converted into usable electricity using geothermal power plants.
Energy that is generated from moving water (such as rivers, waterfalls, or ocean tides) and converted into usable electricity using hydroelectric power plants.
Energy that is generated from organic matter (such as wood, crops, or waste) and converted into usable electricity using biomass power plants.
The release of carbon dioxide and other greenhouse gases into the atmosphere, which contribute to climate change and global warming.
